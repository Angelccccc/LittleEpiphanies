A home

The city didn’t quite wake so much as it stretched—slow and soft—while the first light pooled along the window ledge. Coffee steamed, a small weather system in a chipped mug, and the room settled into that gentle hush where thoughts arrive unannounced.

I wrote a few lines, crossed them out, and smiled at the empty page for being patient. Some days that’s enough: a quiet morning, a promise to try again, and the sense that life keeps unfolding in its own calm tempo.
